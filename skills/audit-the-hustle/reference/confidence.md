# Confidence: the mandatory conclusion format

## Format

The conclusion of every audit must take this shape:

> **X% looks like a real operation, Y% looks like performance / a course-sales funnel.**
> Basis: [the 2–4 signals that moved the number most, each labeled checklist-triggered or own-reasoning]
> Below the waterline (not assessed): [what this audit cannot see]

Rules:
- X + Y = 100. Never 100/0 or 0/100 — those claim verification this framework cannot deliver.
- Strong evidence justifies high confidence (90/10 is fine). The ceiling is on certainty, not on conviction.
- The percentage is a statement about *how far the evidence reaches*, not a prediction of a court finding.

## Above vs below the waterline

**Above the waterline — the framework works here:**
- Anything in the five signal classes: public revenue verification, third-party mentions, negative feedback, operational motion, timeline continuity
- Public claims checked against public records

**Below the waterline — the framework cannot reach:**
- Actual payment volume behind a paywall
- Whether sales/testimonials are self-dealt
- Who actually operates an account
- Private arrangements (sponsorships, undisclosed funding) that change what the public activity means

When the user's real question lives below the waterline, say so in one plain sentence and stop:

> "That's below the waterline — real payment data and self-dealing can't be verified from outside. The signals above tell you everything an outsider can know; the rest is your own read of the person and the circle."

Do not generate a confidence number for a below-the-waterline question. A fabricated percentage on an unreachable fact is the single worst output this skill can produce: it reads as rigor and displaces the reader's own judgment with air.

## Calibration guidance

- Each signal class present and strong: substantial upward movement
- "Unverifiable" classes: no movement either way — say explicitly that they didn't move the number
- One absent class with a plausible mundane explanation: small movement
- Zero negative feedback + heavy success narration: downward movement (clean records are a performance signal)
- The main product appears to be the content about making money, not the thing the money allegedly comes from: strong downward movement, and say this pattern out loud
