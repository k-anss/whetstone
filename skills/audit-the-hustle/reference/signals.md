# Signals: five classes of authenticity evidence

Mark each class **present / absent / unverifiable**. Never collapse "unverifiable" into "absent" — absence of evidence you could have found is a signal; absence of evidence no outsider could find is not.

## 1. Verifiable revenue

What counts:
- Open revenue dashboards wired to a payment processor (not self-reported numbers typed into a page)
- A live product page with a working purchase flow and visible recent activity
- Revenue listings on platforms that verify via API, not screenshots

What does not count:
- Screenshots of dashboards (trivially fabricated)
- Round-number revenue claims in threads or bios
- "Revenue" that is actually the price of the course teaching you to earn that revenue

## 2. Independent third-party mentions

What counts:
- Coverage, reviews, or discussion by people with no follow/affiliate relationship to the subject
- Users complaining or asking support questions in public, unprompted
- Being referenced as a tool/product in unrelated technical discussions

What does not count:
- Retweets and quote-posts from the subject's own circle
- "Featured in" logos with no linkable underlying article
- Affiliate reviews (check for tracking parameters in links)

## 3. Real negative feedback

What counts:
- Specific, mundane complaints (billing glitch, slow support, missing feature) from accounts with their own history
- Refund disputes discussed in public
- Critical reviews the subject responds to defensively or substantively

What does not count:
- A perfectly clean record (real products accumulate real complaints; zero negatives is itself a flag)
- Vague hostile comments from throwaway accounts (could be rivals, tells you nothing)

Why this class matters: shills can manufacture praise at scale; manufacturing a years-deep trail of boring, specific complaints is far more expensive. Mundane negativity is among the cheapest-to-read, hardest-to-fake signals.

## 4. Operational signals in motion

What counts:
- Hiring posts, contractor requests
- Domain/infrastructure changes consistent with a running product (new subdomains, status pages, changelogs)
- Traffic estimates from independent tools trending in a direction consistent with the claims

What does not count:
- A single snapshot of any of these — the signal is *motion over time*, not state

## 5. Timeline continuity

What counts:
- A posting/shipping history that predates the money claims, with consistent texture
- Old content consistent with the skills now claimed
- Gaps that have explanations visible in the record

What does not count:
- An account that woke up recently and immediately began narrating success (revived/purchased account pattern)
- A history that has been visibly pruned

## Output of this step

A five-row table: class | present/absent/unverifiable | one line of what was actually found. This table feeds `confidence.md`. Do not draw the conclusion here.
