# Sample stress test (fictional plan)

> The plan below is invented for demonstration. Names, numbers, and
> platforms are fictional.

## The plan as stated

"I want to sell AI-assisted document services (reports, slide decks,
templates) to local small businesses. Entry channel: a low-priced
listing on a second-hand marketplace. Buyers who convert get added to
my private contact list for higher-value repeat work. I'll spend two
weekends setting it up and expect first revenue within a month."

## Boundary gate

- Emotional distress? No — the user is asking for challenge.
- Already executing? No — setup has not started. Full scope applies.

Enter the role.

## Fatal flaw

The plan's survival depends on inbound search demand existing on that
marketplace for this category. Everything downstream — conversion,
private-list accumulation, repeat work — is unreachable if nobody
searches. The plan contains no test of whether that demand exists; it
assumes it. That is the most probable kill: not bad delivery, but an
empty top of funnel that burns the month silently.

## Hidden assumptions

1. **"Buyers search for this on that platform."** — Verification cost:
   near zero. Search the category yourself; count active listings and
   their visible transaction counts. One evening.
2. **"Buyers can tell my output from cheaper competitors'."** —
   Verification cost: low. Run the listing two weeks; read what
   inquirers actually ask about. If price is the only question, the
   differentiation assumption failed.
3. **"Converted buyers will accept moving to a private channel."** —
   Verification cost: built into the first 3 sales. Track the
   acceptance rate; below 1 in 3, the repeat-work layer is fiction.
4. **"Two weekends of setup is the real cost."** — Verification cost:
   log actual hours in week one. Listing maintenance, inquiry replies,
   and revisions are recurring costs the plan books at zero.

## Multi-round failure

- **1 month**: inquiry replies and revision requests eat evenings; the
  hourly rate quietly drops below the day job's. Energy, not demand,
  becomes the binding constraint.
- **3 months**: the listing format is copied by sellers pricing lower;
  the platform's search ranking favors transaction volume, which the
  copiers accumulate faster.
- **6 months**: the private list holds a handful of one-time buyers
  with no recurring need; "repeat higher-value work" turns out to
  require a different client class than the entry channel attracts —
  the funnel's two ends do not connect.

## Verdict

Conditional. The entry test is cheap and worth running, but only as a
demand probe with a two-week kill criterion (no qualified inquiry in
14 days → close it). Do not pre-build templates or invest the second
weekend before the probe returns.

---

This is the deliberately pessimistic extreme. Take the real judgment
between this and a normal read.
