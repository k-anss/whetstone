# reference/evidence.md — evidence rules, and this skill's own evidence

## Rules for sourcing observations

1. Evidence thickens rules; it never replaces judgment. One witnessed
   failure beats ten paragraphs of argument.
2. Legal sources: the author's own runs, the author's measured data,
   public ecosystem facts the author personally verified.
3. Illegal source: "industry best practices" imported to fill space.
   Imported filler dilutes the judgment density that justified the
   skill's existence.
4. Style: plain physical detail, no drama. Dramatic anecdotes read as
   fabricated; flat specifics read as witnessed.
5. Desensitization: every observation passes the author's redline
   grep before publication (identity, organization, location
   granularity — list supplied by the author).
6. If a rule has no observation behind it yet: ship it thin and say
   so. An honestly thin rule outranks a fabricated thick one.
