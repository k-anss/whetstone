# The delete-test

The single criterion that decides delete-word vs delete-sentence.

## Procedure

1. Take one sentence.
2. Delete every adjective and adverb.
3. Read the remainder.
   - **Stands on its own** (a complete, informative statement) → use the remainder.
   - **A stump** (grammatical fragment, or a statement that says nothing) → the whole sentence was decoration → delete the sentence.

## The line between delete-word and delete-sentence

Ask of the remainder: is what's missing **information**, or only **modification**?

- Only modification missing → the deletion was correct; keep the remainder.
  - "我们精心设计了简洁的导出流程" → "导出流程" (as a label) — stands.
- Information missing, and that information was never in the sentence to begin with → the sentence carried nothing → delete it.
  - "读者将会深刻地感受到机制的精妙之处" → "读者会感受到机制" — a statement that says nothing → delete the sentence.

## Gray zone

If unsure after one pass: also delete honorifics and particles (您/请/哦/呢), then judge again. Sentences that survive both passes are content; sentences that don't were packaging.

## What the test does not judge

- Correctness. A restrained sentence can be factually wrong.
- Completeness. If the text lacks needed information, deletion will not add it — report the gap instead.
