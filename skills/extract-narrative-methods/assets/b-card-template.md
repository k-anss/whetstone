# B Card Template (Management)

Candidate-layer output. B line = a decision-maker with controllable resources
making a meaningful allocation, authorization, incentive, information, or risk
choice. A merely local execution action is not a management card. Six questions
must close or downgrade to an event.

```markdown
### 管理案例 B01：<one-line method-shaped title>

- 章节：<chapters within this chunk>
- 结果状态：已完成 | 阶段完成 | 进行中 | 失败/反噬
- 决策者：<the manager who makes the verifiable choice>
- 管理困境：<the trade-off; why the easy option is wrong>
- 手里有什么：<controllable resources / authority / information>
- 要什么：<the management objective>
- 愿意牺牲什么：<what certainty or option is given up>
- 备选方案：<the alternatives not taken>
- 决策：<the actual decision>
- 执行响应：<how subordinates/system responded — over time, observed>
- 管理逻辑：<the transferable logic; not hindsight>
- 实际代价：<cost actually borne; mark what is NOT the manager's design>
- 信息/立场暴露：<what the decision revealed>
- 遗留风险：<residual risk; no premature "secured/loyal">
- 最强替代解释：<strongest rival explanation, e.g. an external event did the work>
- 预期管理：标⑦〔主导/在场〕 | 不标⑦
- 定位锚：<6–15 字、可在本块原文逐字 grep 到的短片段，仅证明不是编造>
- 关键原句：<表意用，可转述/截断，无需逐字；可留空>
- 方法信号：<management-method signal, not a stable-method declaration>
```

Do not credit an externally imposed condition (an accident, a third party's
independent choice) to the manager just because it benefited them — establish who
designed and controlled it first. Separate proposal, adoption, execution, and
result; do not assign a multi-actor outcome entirely to the proposer.
